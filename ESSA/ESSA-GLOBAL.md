## Institutional Declaration

> The **Earth Safety and Security Agency (ESSA — Global Tier)** is the civil planetary-level authority for lifecycle governance of safety-critical, security-sensitive, and environmentally accountable systems.
>
> Operating under the **United Nations** as its institutional principal, ESSA-Global scales the safety-first constitutional architecture beyond the European domain to encompass all sovereign actors, regional safety bodies, and global technology systems.
>
> *ESSA-Global advances in safety. ESSA-Global protects in security. ESSA-Global sustains for Earth.*

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

ESSA-Global extends the ESSA constitutional lifecycle governance architecture to the **planetary scale** under United Nations authority.

It provides:

- A common constitutional substrate for global civil safety governance
- Federated interoperability between regional ESSA-class agencies (European, national, and multilateral)
- A normative framework for governing safety-critical systems that cross national and continental jurisdictions
- A global accountability layer for emerging technologies (autonomous systems, space operations, quantum technologies, AI-governed infrastructure)

**ESSA-Global does not:**

- Replace or subordinate existing sovereign safety agencies
- Create binding international law unilaterally
- Govern military or defence systems

**ESSA-Global does:**

- Define a common governance protocol for civil lifecycle artefacts across participating jurisdictions
- Establish minimum constitutional invariants applying globally to safety-critical systems
- Provide a federated attestation layer enabling cross-border certification recognition
- Enforce ethical invariants in AI-assisted and autonomous systems at global scale

## 3. Constitutional Invariants (Global Scale)

ESSA-Global establishes seven planetary-scale constitutional invariants. All participating regional and national bodies **SHALL** comply.

| ID | Invariant | Scope |
|----|-----------|-------|
| **GCI-001** | Safety Primacy | Safety defines mission objectives globally — no economic, political, or operational objective overrides defined safety envelopes |
| **GCI-002** | Security as Governance | Security exists to protect safety invariants — never to circumvent, override, or redefine them |
| **GCI-003** | Traceability Universality | All safety-critical lifecycle artefacts **SHALL** carry traceable, machine-verifiable provenance across jurisdictional boundaries |
| **GCI-004** | Human Override Guarantee | No autonomous system **SHALL** operate without a defined and accessible human override path, enforceable across all jurisdictions |
| **GCI-005** | Ethical Non-Maleficence | AI-assisted and autonomous systems **SHALL NOT** be activated against civilian populations, sustainable infrastructure, or protected ecosystems |
| **GCI-006** | Interoperability by Protocol | Certification and conformance attestations **SHALL** be mutually recognisable across ESSA-affiliated agencies via governed protocol exchange |
| **GCI-007** | Sustainability Accountability | All lifecycle systems **SHALL** maintain measurable ESG accountability, auditable at the planetary level |

## 10. Relationship with Regional Agencies

ESSA-Global does **not** absorb or supersede regional ESSA-class agencies. It provides:

- A **constitutional overlay** that regional agencies can adopt voluntarily or through treaty obligation
- **Federated recognition protocols** enabling cross-border certification acceptance
- **Minimum global invariants** (GCI-001 through GCI-007) that regional agencies **SHALL** meet
- A **global arbitration mechanism** for cross-jurisdictional lifecycle disputes

Regional agencies retain full sovereign authority within their jurisdictions, subject only to the global constitutional invariants.

```
ESSA-Global (UN Constitutional Overlay)
```
