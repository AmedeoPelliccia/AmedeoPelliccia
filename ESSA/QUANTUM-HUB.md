## Minimal Charter Statement

> The **Quantum HUB** is the Naples-anchored operational coordination layer that binds ESSA quantum ecosystems — QPU access, NBT gate operations, Quantum Simplicial Neural Network (QSN) pipelines, and quantum-classical bridge services — into a single federated point of orchestration.
>
> It does **not** replace ACQUA's `L_Q` (Quantum Layer) or QQQ's regime theory; it is the *physical and institutional locus* through which their operational services are coordinated, monitored, and federated to sector implementations (AMPEL360 Q100, AMPEL360 Q10, GAIA-AIR / QAOS).
>
> Quantum HUB operates under Naples-led governance with applicable municipal, Italian national, and European Union requirements applied jointly; the stricter safety or containment control governs where requirements overlap.

## 2. Functional Scope

The Quantum HUB coordinates four operational service families:

1. **QPU Access Coordination** — scheduling, queue management, and provenance tagging for quantum processing requests originating from ACQUA `L_C` / `L_Q` boundaries.
2. **NBT Gate Operations** — bridge and tunnel control for the Neural Network Bridging and Tunneling layer defined in the OPT-IN N-axis, including gate invariants INV-001 through INV-003.
3. **QSN Pipeline Services** — execution coordination for Quantum Simplicial Neural Network inference, persistent homology computations, and topological evidence return paths.
4. **Quantum-Classical Bridge Services** — temporal synchronization, simplicial state preparation, and tunnel-back registration into the classical PATH → MTL pipeline.

The Quantum HUB does **not** itself execute quantum computation. It coordinates, governs, and federates access to such execution.
