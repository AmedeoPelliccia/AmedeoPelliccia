## 1. Purpose

This deliverable specifies the requirements for the **Declaration of Honour**, a mandatory administrative attachment that must accompany the AI-BOOST application submitted via the F6S platform for the Frontier AI Grand Challenge (EuroHPC JU, GA 101135737).

The Declaration of Honour confirms that the coordinating entity and all consortium members satisfy the eligibility, exclusion, and financial regulation criteria established by the European Commission and EuroHPC JU.

---

## 2. Source Document

The Declaration of Honour template must be downloaded from the official AI-BOOST project website:

> **URL:** <https://aiboost-project.eu/>

The template is published by the funding body and must be used without modification to its mandatory clauses.

---

## 3. Completion Requirements

### 3.1 Signatory

The Declaration of Honour must be **completed and signed by the legal representative** of the coordinating entity. The legal representative is the person authorised to enter into legally binding commitments on behalf of the organisation.

### 3.2 Eligibility Confirmations

By signing the Declaration of Honour, the legal representative confirms compliance with **all** of the following eligibility criteria:

| # | Criterion | Reference |
|---|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | The coordinating entity is **established in an EU Member State** or an associated country eligible under the call | Horizon Europe participation rules |
| 2 | The coordinating entity is **under EU control** (not subject to control by a non-EU country or non-EU entity that could compromise security or public order) | Call-specific eligibility conditions |
| 3 | The coordinating entity and all partners are **clear of exclusion grounds** as defined in the Financial Regulation | Regulation (EU, Euratom) 2018/1046, Articles 136–141 |

### 3.3 Financial Regulation Reference

The applicable Financial Regulation is **Regulation (EU, Euratom) 2018/1046** of the European Parliament and of the Council of 18 July 2018 on the financial rules applicable to the general budget of the Union. Key provisions relevant to the Declaration of Honour include:

- **Article 136** — Exclusion criteria (bankruptcy, fraud, professional misconduct, tax/social security obligations, etc.)
- **Article 137** — Rejection from a specific award procedure
- **Article 138** — Administrative and financial penalties
- **Article 139** — Remedial measures
- **Article 140** — Publication of information on exclusion and financial penalties
- **Article 141** — Central database (Early Detection and Exclusion System — EDES)
